Getting started

Ankayma puts your machines on a private mesh where they reach each other directly — no VPN, no public ports — and you can prove the vendor was never in the middle. No credit card: signing in creates your own free F0 space.

Your first win — NoKey SSH from your phone into your laptop (5 minutes)

SSH from your iPhone into your MacBook — no key, no bastion, no public port, and you'll Prove It at the end.

The MacBook is the target — the machine you'll reach. ~2 min

  1. Install the Ankayma desktop app and open it — you'll land on the welcome screen.
  2. Click GitHub and approve access in the browser. The app signs you in automatically; this first sign-in creates your free F0 space. (No browser? Click "Enter a token instead" and paste the session token.)
  3. On Devices, click Connect. macOS asks for your password once — a network device needs admin rights.
  4. You'll see "You're in the mesh" with your node address. The target is live.

The iPhone is the client — where you'll type. It joins the same account. ~1 min

  1. On the MacBook, go to Devices → Add a device → show a QR.
  2. On the iPhone, install Ankayma and tap Scan QR on the welcome screen.
  3. Scan the MacBook's QR. The iPhone enrols into the same account — no second GitHub login.
  4. Tap Connect. Both machines are now on your mesh.

NoKey SSH — a shell on your laptop, from your phone, with no static key. ~1 min

  1. On the iPhone, open Services.
  2. Find your MacBook and tap SSH.
  3. You're in a shell on your laptop — no bastion, no static key, no public port. Every session lands in your audit ledger.

Prove It — confirm the vendor was never on the wire. ~30 s

  1. Back on Services, tap ◈ path next to the MacBook.
  2. Read the path chain: direct peer-to-peer, the real endpoint, and Vendor in path: No ✓.
  3. You just proved the session went straight between your two devices — see Prove it for what each row means.

◈ Proof: no bastion, no static key, no public port — and the path chain shows the vendor was never on the wire.

Reaching a server (a VPS or home box) works the same way: install the agent there so it becomes another target, then SSH into it from any of your devices.

Prefer a browser app? Instead of SSH, reach an internal web app with NoVPN — a private name only your mesh resolves, opened in your browser. See Subdomains.

Press “Prove it.”

Most overlays can tell you it connected. None can hand you proof your traffic never touched the vendor. Ankayma shows the real path as an artifact you hold — not a promise you trust.

Honest about the edges: at F0 the receipt is tamper-evident (anchored in an append-only ledger). The ledger records connection-level facts — which run, which identity, which service it reached — not the commands run inside a session. Signing it with your own key plus an external witness is on the roadmap (Growth). Path-proof is absolute for direct peer-to-peer connections. See our honest limits →
