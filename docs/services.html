Services & private domains

Put your app or demo on a branded private name that only the people you invite can reach — no VPN, no public port. This is NoVPN.

Give your service a private name

Turn something running on your machine — a demo, a dashboard, an internal tool — into a clean branded address like demo.you.int.ankayma.com. The name resolves only on devices you've invited, and the traffic goes straight over the mesh. There is no public DNS record and no open port, so outsiders can't even discover it.

Two ways to publish

  • Publish a sample demo — one click. Ankayma serves a ready-made page on a private name, so you have something to show a client instantly — no server to run, no terminal.
  • Point at your own app — already running a web app on your machine? Create a subdomain and point it at that local port. That's your real product, on a private name.

Services — what you can reach

The Services screen is your catalog: the private services you're allowed to reach right now. For a client you invited, it shows exactly the demo you shared — nothing else. Access is default-deny: people see only what you grant.

The point for your business

Show a prospect or partner a private, professional preview of your work — they open it themselves, and nothing is exposed to the public internet. That's the necessary first half of a private client demo; the other half is inviting them without a password (NoPass).
