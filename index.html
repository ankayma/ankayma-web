How it works

From install to proof in 5 minutes

Pick what you want to prove first. Each path ends in proof you hold — not a promise you trust. Connection is a commodity; the receipt is the product.

Your service on your own domain — only your mesh can see it. No public DNS, no open port, and your client still opens the demo themselves.

1~3 minSign in with GitHub and enroll your devices — app on your laptop or phone, CLI on the machine that runs your service. Keys are generated locally and never leave the device; each one joins your mesh with a stable overlay IP.

Deploy from CI with no static secret — every run ends in a signed receipt you hold.

1~3 minSign in with GitHub and enroll your prod box — one CLI command. Keys are generated locally; your device is your admin identity, no secret to store.
2~1 minSet the rule from your laptop or phone: agent ci-policy add --repo you/app --ref main --target prod. "This repo, this branch, deploys to this box."
3~1 minPush. Your Action mints a short-lived OIDC token — scoped, deploy-only, expires in minutes, nothing static in CI — and the signed run-receipt lands in your tamper-evident ledger.

Press “Prove it.”

Most overlays can tell you it connected. None can hand you proof your traffic never touched the vendor. Ankayma shows the real path as an artifact you hold — not a promise you trust.

Honest about the edges: at F0 the receipt is tamper-evident (anchored in an append-only ledger). The ledger records connection-level facts — which run, which identity, which service it reached — not the commands run inside a session.
