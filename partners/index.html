Three ways to help Ankayma grow.

If you believe in a sovereign mesh and want more teams to have it, there's a lane for you — and you're probably already standing in one. They differ by one thing: how much you take on. Read down until one sounds like you.

1

Refer a peer

You already use Ankayma, and someone you know would benefit.

How it works: send them your link. No selling, no paperwork, no gate — just a good pointer between people who run real infrastructure.

What you both get: extra months of service when they actually get going. It's account credit, not cash — the lightest way to help, and the one that keeps community referrals honest.

Get your referral link
2

Champion it inside your company

You're a developer, SRE, or platform lead who wants to bring Ankayma into your own organization from the inside — bottom-up.

How it works: you run it with your team on the free and team tiers and become the internal champion. Because you're now representing Ankayma inside an account, this lane starts with a short partner conversation — we set it up together so credit is clear.

What you get: recognized on the deal when your seed grows into a paying team. This lane can pay — which is exactly why it runs through the partner gate rather than casual referral. Specifics we agree with you directly.

Start a partner conversation
3

Open a door, or co-sell

You can introduce a decision-maker — a CISO or head of IT — or your company sells a complementary product (hardware, network gear) to the same customers. Top-down.

How it works: you open the door or sell alongside your own product; we register the opportunity so your introduction is protected, and recognize it when the deal closes. Co-sell, yes — reseller, no: we keep the product relationship with the customer direct.

What you get: rewarded on close, terms agreed directly — and, over time, something less visible: we help sharpen how your team reads and reports what a customer actually needs. That skill compounds across every deal you bring.

Start a partner conversation

One rule keeps this clean

Cash rewards flow only through the partner lanes (2 and 3), where there's a real deal and a real relationship. Casual peer referrals are always account credit — because paying cash for community referrals buys a few and poisons the well. That single line is why the lanes are separate.

The partner program is still being finalized — a couple of registration details are being settled before the full sign-up opens. Reach out now and we'll bring you in as we open it. And this is a relationship, not a reseller channel: we onboard partners carefully, on purpose.
